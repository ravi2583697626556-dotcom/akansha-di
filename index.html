A Letter for Akansha Di

With all the warmth I couldn't fit in a DM

My dearest Akansha di,

Some people come into your life through years of shared classrooms, family gatherings, and growing up side by side. And then there's Instagram — where a scroll, a follow, a small "hey, loved your post" can quietly begin something that feels just as real.

Just a month ago, I didn't know you existed. And now here I am, writing you a whole letter — because somehow that's where we are, and I wouldn't change it for anything. It's funny, how quickly someone can go from a stranger on the internet to someone you actually look forward to hearing from.

"Not every sister is born in the same home.
Some are found in the smallest moments —
a comment, a reply, a gentle hello —
and they stay, quietly, like sunlight through trees."

Calling you "di" doesn't feel like a formality to me. It feels like something that just fits. There's a warmth in the way you talk, the way you share pieces of your world — and I find myself genuinely looking up to you, in that quiet way you do with someone who just seems to have it together without even trying to show it.

I hope this little page finds you somewhere soft and golden — maybe sitting in sunlight, maybe smiling with your eyes closed the way you do in this photo, completely unbothered by the world. That version of you is my favourite. Just happy. Just present. Just yourself.

Here's to more conversations that run longer than expected, more voice notes, more moments where I think — "it's so nice to have someone like her around." The best people don't always come wrapped in the way we expect. Sometimes they come through a follow request. And you, Akansha di, were a very, very good surprise.

With all the love and warmth I have,

Your Chota Sibling 🌿
